The Walk-In Script That Got Me Hired

Walk in between 2–4pm (after lunch rush, before dinner prep).
Ask for the manager. Say this:

"Hi, I'm looking for a server/busser/host position. Is the manager available for just 5 minutes?"

That's it. Don't overthink it. The key details:

  • 2–4pm is the window — never during rush hours
  • Dress like you could work there that day
  • Bring a paper resume — managers in hospitality still respect it
  • Be specific about the role you want — "I'm looking for work" sounds desperate; "I'm looking for a server position" sounds like you know what you're doing

I hit 15 restaurants in 3 days. Got called back by 4. Hired on day 3. It works.

The Fastest $500: Gig Stack

If you need income in the next 7 days, this is the stack:

  • DoorDash — sign up today, active in 24-48 hrs. Peak hours: lunch and dinner. $20+/hr in a busy neighborhood.
  • Facebook Marketplace — go to thrift stores, flip items. $0 investment if you find free stuff on OfferUp/Craigslist. $200–500 in a weekend.
  • Handy or TaskRabbit — cleaning and odd jobs. Sign up this week, start next week.

Bartending is the highest leverage role. Takes 3–6 months to break in, but $40–80/hr in cash tips changes everything. Start as a barback or server in a bar, work your way behind the stick.

Resume for Hospitality (What Works)

  • One page, clean format, no fancy design
  • List any food/service experience first — even if it was in another country or years ago
  • Certifications matter: NYC Food Handler Certificate ($15, online, same-day) makes you hireable at more places
  • Add "Available immediately" at the top — hospitality hires fast and wants fast starters
