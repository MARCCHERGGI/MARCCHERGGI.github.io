The Real Upfront Cost

The biggest shock for new arrivals: it's not just first month's rent.

Standard NYC requirement: First month + last month + security deposit = 3 months rent upfront.
On a $1,400/month apartment: you need $4,200 ready before you sign anything.

On top of that: if you find the apartment through a broker (and most listings use brokers), add 15% of annual rent as a broker fee. On a $1,400/month place, that's another $2,520.

Total potential upfront cost before you've spent a single night there: $6,720.

Red Flags — Walk Away If You See These

  • Cash only, no lease — no legal protection, you can be kicked out anytime
  • "No background check required" — often means illegal sublet
  • Landlord can't show you the lease before signing — never sign blind
  • Price significantly below market — it's a scam or there's a major problem
  • No heat/AC listed — ask explicitly; in NYC landlords are legally required to provide heat between Oct 1–May 31
  • Broker fee charged by landlord (not agent) — illegal since 2020; don't pay it

The Walk-Through Checklist

  • Test every faucet and shower — water pressure matters
  • Check cell signal in the actual room
  • Open the stove — is there a gas line?
  • Check windows — do they open and lock?
  • Ask about roaches — building or apartment-level?
  • Check basement if it exists — flooding history?
  • What's the noise level at 10am on a weekday vs weekend?
